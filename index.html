About Threat Aware

Threat Aware is an educational project that explains how social engineering works, why it is dangerous, and how people can protect themselves from manipulation online.

The goal is to help users recognize scams, psychological tricks, and deceptive tactics used by attackers on social media, email, and messaging platforms.

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a type of cyber manipulation where attackers trick people into giving away sensitive information, money, or access to accounts.

Instead of hacking computers, criminals often hack human behavior by exploiting trust, fear, curiosity, or urgency.

Common Examples of Social Engineering

  • Phishing emails – Fake emails that look like banks or companies.
  • Fake social media accounts – Pretending to be a trusted person.
  • Romance scams – Building fake emotional relationships for money.
  • Urgent messages – “Your account will be deleted today!”
  • Impersonation – Pretending to be tech support or government officials.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never share passwords with anyone.
  • Check email addresses carefully.
  • Do not click suspicious links.
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests.
  • Use two-factor authentication (2FA).

Conclusion

Social engineering is one of the biggest cybersecurity threats today. By staying informed and cautious, people can significantly reduce their risk of being manipulated.

Threat Aware encourages critical thinking and digital safety in everyday online life.
